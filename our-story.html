Our Story

A journey started with eight laptops and a vision for change.

Ozzy Africa began with a simple question, how can young people build futures when they lack access to technology?

With only eight laptops and a deep commitment to creating opportunity, our founders trained the first cohort of young people in Mbale. The demand grew quickly, revealing the scale of digital exclusion and e-waste accumulation.

Today, our model integrates digital skills training, repair and reuse, solar-powered infrastructure and job creation pathways. We remain rooted in community innovation with a focus on the circular economy.
